Sri Lanka upcountry: A land of lush hills, misty mountains, and ancient tea plantations

Sri Lanka upcountry is a region located in the central highlands of the island. It is known for its lush hills, misty mountains, and ancient tea plantations. Sri Lanka upcountry is a popular tourist destination for those seeking to escape the heat and humidity of the coast and experience the island's natural beauty.
One of the most popular tourist destinations in Sri Lanka upcountry is the city of Kandy. Kandy is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to the Temple of the Tooth Relic, one of the most sacred Buddhist temples in the world. Kandy is also a great base for exploring the surrounding countryside, including the Knuckles Mountain Range and the Sinharaja Forest Reserve.
Another popular tourist destination in Sri Lanka upcountry is the town of Nuwara Eliya. Nuwara Eliya is known for its tea plantations, colonial architecture, and cool climate. Nuwara Eliya is a great place to relax and enjoy the scenery. It is also a good base for hiking in the Horton Plains National Park.
If you are looking for an adventure, Sri Lanka upcountry is also home to a number of white-water rafting and kayaking companies. You can also go mountain biking or hiking in the Knuckles Mountain Range.
Here are a few tips for planning your trip to Sri Lanka upcountry:

  • The best time to visit Sri Lanka upcountry is during the dry season, which runs from December to April.
  • Sri Lanka upcountry can be quite cold at night, so be sure to pack a jacket
  • If you are planning on doing any hiking, be sure to wear comfortable shoes and bring plenty of water.

If you are looking for a relaxing and unforgettable vacation, Sri Lanka upcountry is the perfect destination. With its lush hills, misty mountains, ancient tea plantations, and rich culture, Sri Lanka upcountry has something to offer everyone.
